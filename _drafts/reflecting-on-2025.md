## A year of freelancing

Spent the whole year freelancing with long term gigs with two main clients.

Started late 2024, after a meh year with my last salaried gig. And did a handful of interviews that were quite brutal. Two teams reached out and asked each for a part-time comitment.

## Professional wins

- a good first year that gives me a year of runaway
- two clients with which I built and am building a relationship of trust
- a steep progression tecnically and product-wise speaking : kept digging into Ruby and Rails, discovered and worked a lot with Typesense, started learning the modern front-end side of Rails w/ Hotwire.
- a lot of problems solved and features shipped with little to no perturbations for end users.
- joined a generalist coop that ported my activity and to which i delegated my most of my admin work (and graet people in it)
- from the get go, i worked to have a freelancing practice that is shipshape: time tracking, invoicing, note taking about clients and projects, tasking, etc... this allowed me to be very tranquille all year round, focusing on delivering good software to the teams

## A look on the software engineering market

Whether salaried or freelance work is nasty out there. But i also realized that a lot of the companies that were hot a few years ago, now have stupid expectations for candidates but not some much for them. And this creates a power vacuum where coompanies that were not sexy next to unicorns are now able to snatch good people. And good people are able to work with smaller companies with cool products and often better financial solvability. 

## Manifesting for 2026

- learning from the ground up HTML and CSS so i get better tooling and a deeper apreciation for these languages
- investing time, money and love on my freelancing practice: work on website, make my value proposition clearer, write more, get some help on defining offers that are not stricltyl coupled to my time, meeting people as people (in the age of llms and rat race of content, i think that genuine connections will be the future)
